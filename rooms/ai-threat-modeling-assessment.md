# TryHackMe Room: AI Threat Modeling Assessment

**Category:** Threat Modeling / AI Security
**Status:** Completed

---

## Objective

The goal of this room was to understand how threat modeling applies to AI systems — identifying, categorizing, and documenting security risks in an AI-focused environment.

---

## Tools and Techniques Used

- Threat modeling methodology
- Risk identification
- AI security concept review
- Scenario analysis
- Security control mapping
- Structured documentation
- Critical thinking

---

## Methodology

I started by reviewing the AI-related scenario and mapping out the risks that could affect the system, its users, the underlying data, or the model's behavior.

The assessment followed a structured process:

1. Understand the AI system or scenario being assessed.
2. Identify key assets — data, models, prompts, users, and outputs.
3. Consider possible threat actors and misuse cases.
4. Think through how vulnerabilities could affect confidentiality, integrity, or availability.
5. Map identified risks to relevant mitigations or security controls.
6. Document findings clearly.

---

## Key Concepts Learned

- AI systems introduce security risks that go well beyond traditional applications.
- Threat modeling helps surface weaknesses before they can be exploited.
- AI-specific risks include prompt injection, data leakage, model misuse, insecure plugins, and unreliable outputs.
- Security controls need to be matched to the specific type of risk being addressed.
- Clear documentation matters — especially when explaining AI security concerns to both technical and non-technical audiences.

---

## Challenges Faced

The biggest challenge was shifting my thinking beyond standard application security and considering the many different ways AI systems can fail or be abused. You can't just look at the model in isolation — you have to think about the data, the prompts, how users interact with it, and how everything integrates together.

---

## Lessons Learned

AI security is bigger than the model itself. The surrounding system — data handling, user permissions, prompts, outputs, and integrations — all factor into a complete risk assessment. This room made that clear in a way that's easy to carry into real-world scenarios.

---

## Interview-Relevant Takeaway

This lab reflects awareness of AI-related security risks and emerging threats, hands-on experience with threat modeling methodology, and the ability to assess and communicate complex security concerns in a structured, clear way.
