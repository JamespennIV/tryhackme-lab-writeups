# TryHackMe Room: Missing Person

**Category:** OSINT / Investigation
**Status:** Completed

---

## Objective

The goal of this room was to practice open-source intelligence and investigative reasoning — using provided clues to locate, verify, and connect information in a missing-person style scenario.

---

## Tools and Techniques Used

- OSINT methodology
- Evidence review
- Keyword searching
- Timeline analysis
- Pattern recognition
- Source verification
- Note-taking
- Logical reasoning

---

## Methodology

I started by reading through the full scenario carefully and identifying the most useful clues before doing anything else. The focus was on building a clear investigative path rather than jumping to conclusions.

The investigation followed a consistent structure:

1. Read the full scenario carefully.
2. Flag names, locations, dates, usernames, or anything that felt out of place.
3. Search for connections between clues.
4. Compare information across sources.
5. Build a timeline where the evidence supports it.
6. Verify findings before treating them as reliable.
7. Document conclusions clearly.

---

## Key Concepts Learned

- OSINT investigations live and die on accuracy and patience.
- Small details — usernames, timestamps, locations, specific wording — can completely shift an investigation.
- Information should always be verified before it's trusted.
- Building a timeline helps organize evidence and keeps the investigation from getting messy.
- Good OSINT work requires both curiosity and discipline in equal measure.

---

## Challenges Faced

The biggest challenge was avoiding tunnel vision. In OSINT work, a clue can look significant but lead nowhere — or worse, send you in the wrong direction entirely. I practiced slowing down, comparing multiple pieces of evidence, and not committing to a conclusion until the picture was clear.

---

## Lessons Learned

This room drove home that OSINT isn't just searching online. It's the full process of collecting, organizing, verifying, and explaining information in a way that holds up. Structured investigation and careful documentation aren't optional — they're what separates a useful finding from a guess.

---

## Interview-Relevant Takeaway

This lab reflects investigative thinking, evidence handling, and the discipline to follow a structured process from start to finish — skills that carry directly into threat intelligence, incident response, fraud investigation, and security analysis roles.
