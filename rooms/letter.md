# TryHackMe Room: Letter

**Category:** Investigation / OSINT
**Status:** Completed

---

## Objective

The goal of this room was to sharpen investigation and information-gathering skills — analyzing clues, pulling out what matters, and following the evidence to a well-supported conclusion.

---

## Tools and Techniques Used

- Careful reading of provided evidence
- Open-source intelligence methodology
- Keyword analysis
- Pattern recognition
- Note-taking
- Evidence correlation
- Logical elimination

---

## Methodology

I started by reviewing everything available and identifying the most important clues before doing anything else. A big part of the process was learning to separate useful evidence from noise.

From there the investigation followed a consistent structure:

1. Review the provided scenario thoroughly.
2. Flag key names, terms, locations, or anything that stood out.
3. Look for patterns or connections between clues.
4. Compare findings against the room objective.
5. Document the reasoning at each step.
6. Confirm the final answer through evidence, not guessing.

---

## Key Concepts Learned

- OSINT investigations require patience and careful reading — rushing leads to missed details.
- Small clues can completely change the direction of an investigation.
- Evidence should be verified before drawing any conclusions.
- Keeping notes is essential when juggling multiple clues at once.
- Good investigation work is equal parts technical skill and structured thinking.

---

## Challenges Faced

The biggest challenge was resisting the urge to lock in on the first clue that seemed relevant. Investigation rooms make it easy to follow one lead too early. The better approach is to collect several pieces of evidence and compare them before deciding what actually matters.

---

## Lessons Learned

This room reinforced the value of slowing down. Instead of rushing toward an answer, I practiced organizing clues, reviewing evidence carefully, and letting the reasoning drive the conclusion rather than the other way around.

---

## Interview-Relevant Takeaway

This lab reflects skills that translate directly to cybersecurity work — analyzing information under uncertainty, documenting reasoning clearly, following evidence methodically, and approaching investigations with structure rather than assumptions. All relevant to threat analysis, OSINT, and incident response.
